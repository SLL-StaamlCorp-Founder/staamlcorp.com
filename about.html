About

Independent security research that surfaced a class of vulnerability, validated by Apple and generalized into a patented framework.

Origin

StaamlCorp was founded by Stanley Lee Linton, a cybersecurity researcher and software engineer based in Lafayette, Louisiana. The company's intellectual property originated from independent security research conducted between January and April 2025.

While studying how Safari handled cached content across security mode transitions, Stanley identified a previously undocumented class of vulnerability — designated LDB-01 (Lockdown-Bypass-01) — in which WebAssembly modules, JavaScript blobs, and Service Worker scripts cached during normal operation remained executable after a device transitioned into Lockdown Mode, Apple's hardened security state for high-risk users.

The finding showed that cached content captured before Lockdown Mode activation could continue executing under the new policy. The pattern persisted across app restarts and device reboots, and pointed to a more general class of issue at the boundary between cache layers and policy transitions.

Discovery Timeline

January – April 2025

Independent security research uncovers the LDB-01 vulnerability class: cached executable content persists and executes in Safari after Lockdown Mode activation on iOS, iPadOS, and macOS.

May 5, 2025

Comprehensive vulnerability report submitted to Apple through their Security Research Program, including proof-of-concept code, reproduction steps, and impact analysis.

June 2025

Apple confirms reproduction of the issue: "We reproduced the issue and are investigating." Remediation planned for Fall 2025. Multi-round technical exchange with Apple Product Security.

August 2025

Apple assigns internal CVE reference webkit-294380 to the vulnerability.

September 15, 2025

Apple ships the fix in iOS 26 and iPadOS 26. Stanley credited in Apple security advisories. Apple issues a monetary recognition award for the disclosure.

April 2026

Utility patent application filed for the generalized, platform-independent framework — 30 claims covering detection, validation, and mitigation across cache layers and policy transitions.

Beyond a single instance

Apple's remediation addressed LDB-01 on their own platform. The underlying class — temporal security discontinuities in cached executable content — is structural, and appears wherever a system caches executables and supports policy transitions: operating systems, browsers, enterprise MDM frameworks, AI sandboxes, and container runtimes among them.

StaamlCorp's patent application describes a generalized architectural framework for that class: detecting policy transitions, computing the policy delta, identifying and validating cached content under the new policy, and mitigating non-compliant items with an audit trail. Apple validated the underlying issue on their platform; StaamlCorp patented the framework that applies across the rest.
